# Topic 17: E-E-A-T & Google Quality Guidelines

## 1. What is E-E-A-T?
E-E-A-T is a major part of Google's Search Quality Rater Guidelines. It stands for:
*   **E - Experience:** Does the author have first-hand, real-world experience with the topic? (e.g., actually using a product before reviewing it).
*   **E - Expertise:** Is the author knowledgeable and highly skilled in this field?
*   **A - Authoritativeness:** Is the website or author recognized as a leading authority in the industry?
*   **T - Trustworthiness:** (The most important) Is the website secure, accurate, and transparent? Do they display terms of service and accurate contact info?

## 2. What are YMYL Topics?
YMYL stands for **"Your Money or Your Life."** 
Google holds YMYL topics to the absolute highest E-E-A-T standards. If your website covers Finance, Medical Advice, Legal Issues, or Safety, Google will heavily scrutinize your E-E-A-T. If you have no proven medical background, a health blog will simply not rank in modern SEO.

## 3. How to Improve Your Site's E-E-A-T
1.  **Detailed Author Bios:** Every article should have a clear author bio highlighting their credentials, experience, and links to their LinkedIn or Twitter.
2.  **Comprehensive 'About Us' Page:** Explain the story of your business, who runs it, and your mission.
3.  **Cite Authoritative Sources:** When stating facts, link out to high-authority sites (like .edu or .gov sites).
4.  **Keep Content Updated:** Regularly update old posts so information remains accurate.
