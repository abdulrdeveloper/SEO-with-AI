# Google Algorithms: Major and Minor Updates

---

## 1. What is a Google Algorithm?
A Google Algorithm is a complex set of rules, formulas, and AI systems that Google uses to determine which websites should rank at the top of the Search Engine Results Pages (SERPs). 
Google updates these rules constantly to filter out spam and reward high-quality content.

---

## 2. Major Google Algorithms
These are the biggest updates that completely changed how SEO works. While the image lists 8, we have rounded it out to 10 by adding two recent crucial AI updates:

1.  **Panda** — Focuses on content quality. Penalizes thin, plagiarized, or low-quality content.
2.  **Penguin** — Focuses on links. Penalizes websites buying fake or spammy backlinks.
3.  **Hummingbird** — Focuses on search intent and understanding the meaning behind words (Semantic search).
4.  **Pigeon** — Focuses on Local SEO. Improved local search results and Google Maps ranking.
5.  **Mobile (Mobilegeddon)** — Penalized sites that were not mobile-friendly.
6.  **RankBrain** — Google's first major machine-learning AI algorithm to guess what users mean.
7.  **Medic** — Heavily affected YMYL (Your Money or Your Life) sites like health and finance, demanding high authority and trust.
8.  **Core Updates** — Broad, massive updates Google rolls out multiple times a year to improve overall search quality.
9.  **BERT** *(Bonus)* — Advanced Natural Language Processing to understand the exact context of sentences.
10. **Helpful Content Update** *(Bonus)* — Rewards content created organically for humans, penalizing AI-generated spam.

---

## 3. Minor Google Algorithms
These updates were smaller or targeted very specific, isolated spam patterns:

1.  **Florida** — One of the earliest updates aimed at stopping massive keyword stuffing.
2.  **Big Daddy** — Improved Google's server infrastructure and handling of URL redirects/canonicals.
3.  **EMD (Exact Match Domain)** — Penalized spammy sites that used keyword-heavy domains (like `buy-cheap-shoes-online.com`) but had zero good content.
4.  **Jagger** — Targeted historical link-building spam and unnatural link exchanges.
5.  **Payday** — Targeted highly spammy queries like payday loans, casinos, and adult sites.
6.  **Vince** — Gave a slight ranking boost to big, recognized brands for broad search terms.
7.  **Caffeine** — Massive rebuild of Google's indexing system, allowing it to crawl and index pages much faster.
8.  **Quality Updates (Phantom)** — Unannounced tweaks to Google's core ranking algorithm regarding core quality.
9.  **Fred** — Targeted sites that prioritized massive ads and affiliate links over actual helpful content.
10. **Page Layout Algorithm** — Penalized sites that had way too many ads "above the fold" (forcing users to scroll down just to see the content).
