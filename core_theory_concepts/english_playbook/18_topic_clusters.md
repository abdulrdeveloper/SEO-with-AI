# Topic 18: Topic Clusters & Content Silos (Website Architecture)

## 1. What are Topic Clusters?
Back in the day, people would write random blog posts about different topics. Today, Google rewards **Topical Authority** (proving you are the ultimate expert on one specific subject). 

A Topic Cluster (or Content Silo) is an SEO strategy where you group related content together. It consists of three parts:
1.  **Pillar Page:** A massive, comprehensive 3000+ word guide covering a broad topic (e.g., "The Ultimate Guide to Digital Marketing").
2.  **Cluster Content:** Smaller, detailed articles answering specific questions related to the broad topic (e.g., "What is SEO?", "How to do Email Marketing").
3.  **Internal Linking:** All the small Cluster articles link back to the main Pillar page, and the Pillar page links out to the Clusters. 

## 2. Why Do Topic Clusters Work?
*   **Search Engine Spider Friendly:** When Google crawls your site, the neat internal linking helps bots find all your content fast.
*   **Passes Link Juice:** If one of your cluster articles goes viral and gets backlinks, that SEO power flows through your internal links and boosts the Pillar Page too.
*   **Builds Expert Status:** It forces you to cover a topic entirely, signaling to Google that your site is the #1 authority in that niche.

## 3. Building a Topic Cluster Strategy using AI
Planning 20-30 interconnected articles manually requires a lot of brainstorming. AI thrives at organizing data and can map out entire six-month content strategies in one go.

### 🤖 Topic Cluster Map AI Prompt
Use this prompt to get a ready-to-write SEO business plan:

```text
Act as a Master SEO Architect and Content Strategist.

I want to build Topical Authority for my website in the following niche/industry: "[INSERT YOUR NICHE, e.g., Indoor Plant Care]".

My primary goal is to create a complete "Topic Cluster" strategy to dominate this niche.

Please generate a structured map including:
1. One Broad "Pillar Page" Title (Targeting a highly competitive short-tail keyword).
2. 10 highly specific "Cluster Page" Titles (Targeting easy-to-rank, long-tail and question-based keywords).
3. The Search Intent for each cluster page (Informational, Transactional, etc.).
4. A brief (1-sentence) description of how the internal linking will flow between these specific pages.

Present the results in a clean, easy-to-read table format.
```
**Why this works:** You will immediately get a logically mapped-out, month-long content calendar. Every post you write will fit perfectly into a master SEO strategy, rather than just writing blindly.
