# Topic 10: Best Tools for Manual Keyword Research

---

## Why Do We Need Keyword Research Tools?

Doing keyword research manually means you need software to tell you what people are actually typing into search engines. These tools provide critical data like:
- **Search Volume:** How many times a keyword is searched per month.
- **Keyword Difficulty (KD):** How hard it is to rank on the first page for that keyword.
- **Related Keywords:** Suggestions and long-tail variations you might not have thought of.

Here are the best tools you can use for manual keyword research, ranging from free to premium:

---

## 1. Google Ads Keyword Planner (Free)
This is Google's official tool, built primarily for advertisers, but it is an absolute goldmine for SEO keyword research.
- **Why use it:** It provides data directly from Google.
- **Best for:** Finding primary keywords, discovering new keyword ideas, and getting rough search volume estimates.
- **How to use it:** You just need a free Google Ads account. Go to "Discover new keywords," type in your main topic, and it will generate hundreds of related ideas.

## 2. Google Trends (Free)
Google Trends doesn't show exact search volumes, but it shows the **popularity of a search term over time**.
- **Why use it:** To check if a keyword is a dying trend, a growing trend, or a seasonal topic.
- **Best for:** Avoiding keywords that are losing popularity and finding out *when* to post certain content.
- **How to use it:** Type multiple keywords to easily compare which one is currently trending higher in your specific country.

## 3. Mangools (KWFinder) (Freemium/Paid)
KWFinder by Mangools is widely considered the most user-friendly keyword research tool on the market, especially for beginners.
- **Why use it:** It has the most accurate "Keyword Difficulty" (KD) score score available.
- **Best for:** Finding **Long-Tail Keywords** with low SEO difficulty that a new website can easily rank for.
- **How to use it:** Type in a seed keyword, and the tool will show you exact search volumes, history, and a color-coded difficulty score (green = easy to rank).

## 4. WordStream Free Keyword Tool
WordStream offers a completely free keyword tool that acts as a great alternative to paid software.
- **Why use it:** It’s fast, doesn't require an account for basic searches, and gives great contextual suggestions.
- **Best for:** Finding industry-specific keywords and tailoring your results based on location and category.
- **How to use it:** Enter your URL or a keyword, and it will provide a list of relevant terms along with competition levels.

## 5. KeywordTool.io & New Keyword Finders (Freemium)
Keyword Tool is famous for scraping the "Google Autocomplete" feature (the suggestions dropdown that appears when you start typing in Google).
- **Why use it:** Sometimes Google Keyword Planner hides long-tail keywords. This tool uncovers them.
- **Best for:** Finding question-based keywords (Who, What, Where, How) and optimizing for other platforms like **YouTube, Amazon, and Bing**.
- **How to use it:** Enter a broad keyword, and it will generate hundreds of long-tail variations and questions people are asking.

---

## Quick Strategy for Manual Keyword Research:
1. Put your main topic in **WordStream** or **Keyword Tool** to get a broad list of ideas.
2. Put the best ideas into **Google Keyword Planner** to verify search volume.
3. Check **Google Trends** to make sure the keyword isn't losing popularity.
4. If you have the budget, plug the final list into **Mangools (KWFinder)** to ensure the Keyword Difficulty is low enough for your site to rank!
