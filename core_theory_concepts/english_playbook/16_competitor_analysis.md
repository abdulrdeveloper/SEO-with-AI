# Topic 16: SEO Competitor Analysis (Stealing Competitor Traffic)

## 1. What is SEO Competitor Analysis?
Competitor analysis is the process of legally "spying" on the websites that are ranking higher than you. Instead of guessing what Google wants, you find the top 3 websites for your keyword and reverse-engineer their exact strategy.

## 2. What Exactly Are We Looking For?
When analyzing competitors, you want to uncover three main secrets:
1.  **Keyword Gaps:** Keywords they are ranking for, but you are not. 
2.  **Backlink Profile (Link Intersect):** Which high-authority websites are linking to them? If a site links to your competitor, they will likely link to you too.
3.  **Content Structure:** How long is their article? What H2 and H3 subheadings do they use? Do they have videos or custom infographics?

## 3. Best Tools for Competitor Analysis
*   **Ahrefs & SEMrush (Paid):** The absolute best tools. You just paste your competitor's URL, and it shows you every single keyword they rank for, how much traffic they get, and a list of all their backlinks.
*   **Moz (Freemium/Paid):** Great for comparing Domain Authority.
*   **Ubersuggest (Free limits):** Good basic overview for beginners to see top competitor pages.

## 4. Using AI for Fast Competitor Content Analysis
You don't need paid tools to do basic content gap analysis. AI can instantly compare your article to your competitor's article and tell you what you missed.

### 🤖 The Content Gap AI Prompt
Find the #1 ranking article for your keyword. Copy its text. Then, copy your own article's text. Paste both into Claude or ChatGPT (models with large context windows work best) using this prompt:

```text
Act as a Senior SEO Strategist. 

I am trying to rank for the keyword: "[INSERT KEYWORD]".
I have provided two articles below. The first is the current #1 ranking competitor's article. The second is my draft article.

Competitor's Article:
[PASTE COMPETITOR TEXT HERE]

My Article:
[PASTE YOUR TEXT HERE]

Please analyze both articles and provide a "Content Gap Analysis":
1. What specific sub-topics, entities, and questions did the competitor cover that I missed?
2. How can I improve my heading structure compared to theirs?
3. Provide 3-5 actionable recommendations on what paragraphs or sections I should add to make my article 10x better and more comprehensive than the competitor's.
```
**Why this works:** It guarantees your content is more comprehensive and informative than the current #1 result, giving Google a strong reason to outrank them.
