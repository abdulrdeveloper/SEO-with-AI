## 6. Off-Page SEO

### 6.1 Link Building — Types and Quality

**What it is:** Getting other websites to link to your pages. Links pass PageRank ("link juice") and are Google's #1 authority signal.

**Link quality factors:**
| Factor | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Domain authority (DR/DA) | Higher authority site = more PageRank passed |
| Relevance | Link from a cooking site to your marketing blog = low signal |
| Placement | Body content links > footer/sidebar links |
| Anchor text | Keyword-rich anchors help but must be diversified |
| Follow vs Nofollow | `rel="dofollow"` (default) passes PageRank; `nofollow` does not |
| Link uniqueness | Link from a site that links to nobody else in your niche = more powerful |

---

### 6.2 White Hat Link Building

**What it is:** Earning links through legitimate, Google-approved methods.

**Methods that work in 2025:**

**1. Digital PR**
- Create original research, data studies, or surveys
- Pitch findings to journalists and bloggers via HARO (now Connectively), Qwoted, Sourcebottle
- One good data study can earn 30–200 natural backlinks

**2. Guest Posting**
- Write articles for relevant blogs in your niche
- Include 1–2 contextual links to your site
- Target sites with real traffic (check Ahrefs → Traffic > 500/mo)
- Avoid pure "guest post networks" — Google penalizes these

**3. Broken Link Building**
- Find broken links on relevant websites (Ahrefs → Site Explorer → Broken Links on competitor sites)
- Create replacement content
- Email the webmaster and suggest your page as a replacement
- Conversion rate: 5–10%

**4. The Skyscraper Technique**
- Find high-performing content with many links
- Create a significantly better version
- Reach out to everyone linking to the original and present your version

**5. Resource Page Links**
- Search: `[your topic] + "useful resources"` or `intitle:"resources" [your keyword]`
- Submit your tool/guide to relevant resource pages

**6. Unlinked Brand Mentions**
- Use Google Alerts or Ahrefs Alerts for your brand name
- Find mentions without links
- Email the author asking them to add a link
- Easiest win — they already like your brand

**7. HARO / Expert Quotes**
- Sign up for Connectively (formerly HARO), Qwoted, or Terkel
- Respond to journalist queries in your niche with data-driven quotes
- Earn links from major publications (Forbes, Entrepreneur, Inc.)

---

### 6.3 Gray Hat Link Building

**What it is:** Tactics in a gray area — not explicitly approved by Google but widely used. Higher risk, faster results.

**Tactics (use at your own risk):**

**Web 2.0s:** Creating content on platforms like Medium, Blogger, Tumblr, or WordPress.com with links back to your site. These have low but real DR. Value has declined since Google's spam updates.

**Social Bookmarking:** Submitting URLs to Reddit, Digg, Mix, Folkd, Scoop.it. Mostly `nofollow` links — brand signal more than PageRank.

**Profile Links / Directory Submissions:**
- Create profiles on industry platforms, forums, and directories with a link in bio
- High-DA directories: Crunchbase, AngelList, G2, Clutch, Yelp, BBB
- These pass minimal PageRank but help with brand authority and indexing

**Tiered Link Building:**
- Build links to your money pages (Tier 1) from quality sites
- Build links to your Tier 1 sites from Web 2.0s and directories (Tier 2)
- Amplifies Tier 1 link power without pointing spam directly at your site
- Higher risk if done sloppily

### 6.4 Anchor Text Diversity

**What it is:** The clickable text of a backlink.

**Why it matters:** Over-optimized anchor text (too many exact-match keyword anchors) is a Penguin penalty trigger.

**Healthy anchor text distribution:**
| Type | Example | Target % |
|---|---|---|
| Branded | "Ahrefs", "Neil Patel" | 40–50% |
| Naked URL | "https://yourdomain.com" | 15–20% |
| Generic | "click here", "read more" | 10–15% |
| Partial match | "keyword research guide" | 10–15% |
| Exact match | "keyword research" | 5–10% |
| LSI/Synonyms | "find keywords", "search intent" | 5–10% |

---

### 6.5 Domain Authority (DA / DR)

**What it is:** Third-party metrics (not Google metrics) that estimate a domain's link authority.

| Metric | Tool | Scale | What it measures |
|---|---|---|---|
| Domain Authority (DA) | Moz | 0–100 | Link profile quality |
| Domain Rating (DR) | Ahrefs | 0–100 | Strength of backlink profile |
| Trust Flow | Majestic | 0–100 | Link quality from trusted seeds |
| Spam Score | Moz | 0–100% | Proportion of spam signals |

**These are proxies — Google doesn't use them.** High DR ≠ rankings guarantee. Use them to benchmark and compare, not as absolute targets.

### Common Mistakes
- Buying links from PBNs (Private Blog Networks) — Google catches these; penalty is severe
- 100% exact-match anchor text — Penguin flag
- Building links to your homepage only — diversify to inner pages
- Link velocity spikes (100 links in a week, then nothing) — looks manipulative
- Accepting links from irrelevant niches — relevance matters as much as authority
- Ignoring `nofollow` links — they still drive traffic and brand signals

### Section Checklist
- [ ] Anchor text distribution is natural (branded > naked > generic > partial > exact)
- [ ] Link building strategy documented (not ad hoc)
- [ ] Monitor new/lost backlinks monthly (Ahrefs → New Backlinks)
- [ ] No links from obvious PBNs (check in Ahrefs for link farms)
- [ ] Disavow file updated if toxic links found
- [ ] Unlinked brand mentions identified and outreach sent
- [ ] Guest post pipeline active with 5+ prospects
