## 7. Local SEO

### 7.1 Google Business Profile (GBP)

**What it is:** The free listing that appears in Google Maps and the local "Map Pack" (3 listings shown above organic results for local queries).

**Why it matters:** Local Pack results get ~44% of clicks for local queries. GBP is the most important local SEO asset.

**Optimization checklist:**
1. **Claim and verify** your listing (postcard, phone, or video verification)
2. **Business name:** Exact legal name — no keyword stuffing ("Joe's Plumbing | Best Plumber NYC" = policy violation)
3. **Categories:** Primary category is most important. Add all relevant secondary categories.
4. **Address:** Must match NAP on your website exactly (format: "123 Main St" not "123 Main Street")
5. **Hours:** Keep up to date including holidays
6. **Description:** 750 chars, include primary service keywords naturally in first 250 chars
7. **Photos:** Add minimum 10 photos — exterior, interior, team, products. Geotagged photos with relevant file names help.
8. **Posts:** Publish GBP Posts weekly (offers, events, updates) — they appear in your panel
9. **Q&A:** Seed your own FAQs and answer them
10. **Reviews:** Ask every happy customer for a review. Respond to all reviews (good and bad) within 48 hours.

**GBP ranking factors (Map Pack):**

Google officially names three: Relevance, Distance, Prominence. Here's what each actually means in practice:

#### Relevance
How well your GBP listing matches the search query.

**What affects it:**
- **Primary category** — the single most impactful GBP field. "Plumber" vs "Emergency Plumber" are different categories with different query eligibility.
- **Secondary categories** — add all relevant ones (a dental clinic can add "Cosmetic Dentist", "Teeth Whitening Service", "Dental Implants Periodontist")
- **Services section** — fill in every service with its own name and description. Google reads these.
- **Business description** — use your top 2–3 service keywords naturally in first 250 chars
- **Products section** — if applicable, list products with names, prices, and descriptions
- **Website on-page signals** — Google reads your linked website. If your site has no content about "boiler repair" but your GBP has that service, relevance signal is weak.

#### Distance
How far your business is from the searcher (or the location implied in the query).

**What affects it:**
- Physical location of your business — non-negotiable; you can't rank in a city you're not in
- **Service Area Business (SAB):** If you're a mobile business, set service areas in GBP. You can rank in those areas even without a storefront, but weaker than businesses physically in that area.
- "Near me" queries: Googlebot uses the searcher's GPS. A user 0.5 miles from your competitor and 2 miles from you — competitor likely wins on distance alone.
- **Proximity hack:** If multiple locations are possible, get a physical address in each target market area (co-working space, legit office) — not P.O. Boxes.

#### Prominence
How well-known and authoritative your business is — both online and offline.

**What affects prominence (with impact level):**

| Signal | Impact | How to improve |
|---|---|---|
| Review count | Very High | Systematic review request process after every job |
| Review rating (avg) | Very High | Respond to all reviews; address negatives |
| Review recency | High | Reviews need to be ongoing — old reviews decay in weight |
| Review keywords | Medium | When customers mention services in reviews, it boosts relevance |
| Backlinks to your website | High | Local links (news, chambers, sponsors) strongest |
| Citation volume & consistency | Medium | NAP must match across all sources |
| GBP post frequency | Medium | Weekly posts signal active, maintained listing |
| Photo count & recency | Medium | Listings with 100+ photos get 520% more calls (Google data) |
| Q&A activity | Low–Medium | Seed your own Q&As; answer all user questions |
| Website authority (DA/DR) | Medium | Linked website's authority contributes to prominence |
| Engagement signals | Medium | Calls, direction requests, website clicks from GBP all matter |

**Review velocity matters:** Getting 50 reviews in one week looks manipulative. A steady 5–10 reviews/month over a year is far more powerful. Google filters review spikes.

**Review response strategy:**
- Positive reviews: thank them, mention service performed (adds keyword context)
- Negative reviews: acknowledge, don't argue, offer to resolve offline. This is read by future customers, not just Google.

**Example responses:**
```
Positive: "Thank you [Name]! We're glad our emergency boiler repair 
service got your heating back on quickly. Call us anytime!"

Negative: "We're sorry to hear about your experience [Name]. 
This isn't our standard. Please call us at [phone] so we can 
make this right — we'd like to resolve this for you directly."
```

### 7.1.1 Map Pack SERP Features Worth Knowing

**Local Pack (3-Pack):** Standard 3 listings. Requires GBP + local relevance.

**Local Pack with Ads:** Google Local Service Ads (LSAs) appear above the organic 3-pack. Pay-per-lead, background-checked badge. Separate from GBP optimization but amplifies visibility.

**Justifications:** Small snippets below GBP listings quoting why this result is shown — from reviews ("great plumber"), from your website, or from GBP attributes. You can influence these by:
- Having keyword-rich reviews
- Adding attributes to GBP (e.g., "women-led", "LGBTQ+ friendly", "wheelchair accessible")
- Having the keyword on your website pages

**Knowledge Panel:** When someone searches your brand directly, a full panel appears on the right. Keep GBP complete to fill this out.

### 7.2 NAP Consistency

**What it is:** Name, Address, Phone number — must be identical across your website and all citations.

**Why it matters:** Inconsistent NAP confuses Google about which information is accurate, weakening local authority.

**Rules:**
- Pick one format and stick to it everywhere: "Suite 100" vs "Ste. 100" vs "#100" — pick one
- Same phone number format: "(212) 555-0100" vs "212.555.0100" — pick one
- Embed NAP in your website footer using LocalBusiness schema markup

**Audit tool:** BrightLocal → Citation Tracker, or Whitespark → Citation Finder

---

### 7.3 Citations

**What it is:** Mentions of your business NAP on other websites — directories, review sites, data aggregators.

**Priority citation sources:**
| Tier | Sources |
|---|---|
| Data Aggregators | Data Axle, Neustar Localeze, Foursquare |
| Top Directories | Yelp, Apple Maps, Bing Places, Facebook, Yellow Pages |
| Industry-Specific | Healthgrades (medical), Avvo (legal), Houzz (home services), TripAdvisor (restaurants) |
| Local Sources | Local Chamber of Commerce, local newspaper sites, city directories |

**Building citations:**
- Manual: submit to each site individually
- Tools: BrightLocal Citation Builder, Whitespark Citation Building Service, Yext (expensive, auto-syncs)

### 7.4 Local Content & On-Page Signals

- Include city/region keywords naturally in title tags, H1s, and body content
- Create individual pages for each service area (if you serve multiple cities)
- Embed a Google Map on your Contact page
- Get local backlinks: sponsor local events, partner with local businesses, get listed in local news

**AI prompt for local SEO:**
```
Act as a local SEO expert. I run a [business type] in [City, State].

2. Create a review request script (SMS and email version) asking customers to

   with their URLs

   Address: [address]

   Website: [URL]
   Hours: [hours]
   Category: [category]

5. Write 5 city-specific H1 and title tag variations for a service page targeting 
   "[service] in [city]"
```

### Common Mistakes
- Stuffing keywords into the GBP business name
- Different phone numbers or address formats across citations
- Not responding to negative reviews (public trust signal)
- Creating multiple GBP listings for one location
- Using a P.O. Box as the business address (against GBP guidelines)
- No local schema markup on the website
- Ignoring photos — listings with 100+ photos get dramatically more clicks
