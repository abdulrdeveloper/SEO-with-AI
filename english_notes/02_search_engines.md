# What is a Search Engine & How Does it Work?

## 1. What is a Search Engine?
A search engine is like a huge smart library on the internet. When you ask it a question (by typing in the search bar), it goes through billions of web pages to find the best and most accurate answers for you.

## 2. How Does a Search Engine Work?
It works in 4 simple steps:
1. **Crawling:** The search engine sends out tiny programs called "bots" or "spiders" to scan websites on the internet. They look for new pages, images, and videos.
2. **Indexing:** Once scanned, it stores and organizes this information in its massive database (called an Index). Think of it like adding a new book to a library's catalog.
3. **Ranking:** When you search for something, it quickly goes through its index to find the most relevant results and orders them from best (#1) to worst based on lots of rules (algorithms).
4. **Displaying Results (SERP):** Finally, it displays these ranked results to you on the screen, which is what we call the Search Engine Results Page (SERP).

## 3. Popular Search Engines (Global & Local)
Not everyone in the world uses Google! Depending on the country, people use different search engines:
*   **Global Boss:** **Google** (Controls more than 90% of searches worldwide).
*   **Microsoft's Engine:** **Bing** (Second most popular, built into Windows).
*   **For Privacy:** **DuckDuckGo** (Famous because it doesn't track what you search).
*   **China:** **Baidu** (Google is blocked in China, so everyone uses Baidu).
*   **Russia:** **Yandex** (Very popular in Russia and surrounding countries).
*   **South Korea:** **Naver** (Heavily used by Koreans).
