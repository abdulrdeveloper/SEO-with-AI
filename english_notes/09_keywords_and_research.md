# Topic 9: What are Keywords, Their Types, and Keyword Research

---

## 1. What are Keywords?

Keywords are the words or phrases that people type into Google or any other search engine to find something.

> **Simple Example:**
> If you want to buy new shoes, you might type *"men's running shoes"* into Google — that is a **keyword**. The job of SEO is to ensure that when someone types this, your website appears at the very top.

---

## 2. Types of Keywords

Keywords are divided based on their **length** and the **user's intent**.

---

### A. Based on Length

#### Short-tail Keywords
- **What they are:** Keywords consisting of 1 or 2 words.
- **Search Volume:** Very high (millions of people search for them).
- **Competition:** Very high — extremely difficult to rank.
- **Examples:** `"Shoes"`, `"SEO"`, `"Laptop"`

> **If you purely focus on these:** It is almost impossible for a new website to rank for these keywords — big brands are already dominating Google for them.

---

#### Long-tail Keywords
- **What they are:** Keywords consisting of 3 or more words.
- **Search Volume:** Low — but **highly targeted**.
- **Competition:** Low — easier to rank for.
- **Examples:** `"Men's Nike running shoes size 10"`, `"Technical SEO checklist for beginners"`

> **Tip:** Always start with long-tail keywords for a new website. The traffic might be slightly lower, but the visitors you do get will **convert** — because the user is looking for exactly that item or answer.

---

#### LSI (Latent Semantic Indexing) Keywords
- **What they are:** Words related and semantically similar to your main keyword.
- **What they do:** They help Google understand what your page is actually about.
- **Examples:** If your main keyword is `"Apple"`, LSI keywords would be: `iPhone`, `MacBook`, `App Store`, `Tim Cook`.

> **If you don't use them:** Google might get confused — is your page about the "Apple" fruit or the company? LSI keywords remove this ambiguity.

---

### B. Based on User Intent

There is always an **intent (purpose)** behind what a user searches. Understanding this intent is the most important thing in SEO:

| Intent Type | What the User Wants | Example |
|-------------|---------------------|-------|
| **Informational** | To learn or know about something | `"how to bake a cake"` |
| **Navigational** | To go to a specific website | `"Facebook login"` |
| **Commercial** | To compare or research options | `"best laptops 2025"` |
| **Transactional** | Ready to buy immediately | `"buy iPhone 15 Pro Max"` |

> **Important Note:** If you run an e-commerce site, **Transactional** and **Commercial** keywords are the most valuable for you. For blogs, **Informational** keywords are the best.

---

## 3. How to Do Keyword Research?

This is a step-by-step process that helps you find the right keywords:

**Step 1 — Brainstorming**
Think about what your ideal user would type. Ask yourself: *"If I had to find this thing, what would I search for?"*

**Step 2 — Start with Free Tools**
- **Google Keyword Planner** — completely free, Google's own tool.
- **Ubersuggest** — excellent for beginners.
- **Google Search "People Also Ask"** — a goldmine, do not ignore it.

**Step 3 — Paid Tools (When you have a budget)**
- **Ahrefs** — the absolute best tool in the industry.
- **SEMrush** — fantastic for competitor analysis.

**Step 4 — Spy on Competitors**
Go to your top competitors' websites and see what topics they are writing about. Read their headings and meta titles — the best keywords are often hidden there.

**Step 5 — Create the Right Mix**
Don't stick to just one type. A good keyword strategy should include:
- 2-3 Short-tail keywords (for long-term goals)
- 8-10 Long-tail keywords (for quick wins)
- LSI keywords naturally woven throughout the content

The fastest and most accurate keyword research can now be done using AI — you just need to provide your website's code.

- **LMSYS Chatbot Arena** — lmarena.ai (You can select Claude Opus or GPT-4o here)

**What to Do:**
Copy the entire code from your `index.html` file and paste it into the AI along with the prompt provided below. There's no need to manually explain your website — the AI will understand by reading the code.

**AI Keyword Research Prompt:**


## Quick Summary

| Concept | Key Point |
|-------|-----------|
| Short-tail keywords | High volume, high competition — good long-term goal. |
| Long-tail keywords | Low competition, high conversion — best for new sites. |
| LSI keywords | Build topical authority and make content look natural. |
| User intent | Most important — targeting the wrong intent brings traffic but no conversions. |
| AI research | Feed your website's code to AI, and it will generate the best keywords. |
